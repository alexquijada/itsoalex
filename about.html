about Alex

I am an Arizona native and Arizona State University Graduate. Following my graduation from ASU, I decided to apply for graduate school at the University of Arizona and enrolled in their architecture program. After 2 1/2 years, I decided that though I have a passion for architecture, my true calling was designing for the user's experience. That journey led me to Lexington, Kentucky and my first job in the state as a Content Administrator/Graphic Designer at the University of Kentucky for the Student Success website.

During my time at UK, I developed the site map and userflows, re-structured the information architecture, created graphics for the site, all in the hopes of making the site more user friendly for students.

My 5 years in architecture have molded me to think of the user's needs first and design second. User experience in architecture is very similar to UX in mobile and desktop spaces. Designing the lobby in a building is very similar to designing the first screen of an app or the homepage of a website. You want the user to have a pleasant experience so that they keep coming back and enjoying the space.
